Snapchat forgets everything. Instagram forgets nothing. But a place — a festival, a campus, a downtown block — has a living memory of what's happening right now. Sonar is that layer. If you're somewhere busy, you have one question: is it worth going over there, right now? No feed answers that — they're all timeless. The people who feel this most are festival-goers, students, conference crowds — anyone in a dense, time-sensitive place. You drop a waypoint — a note, a photo, a video — onto a channel, and you choose how long it lives. Here's the trade: life and size pull against each other. A fat fifty-megabyte video burns out in fifteen minutes; a tiny note can linger a full day. It's a byte-hour budget — the longer something lives, the lighter it has to be. And once it's live, every like buys it five more minutes, so the crowd keeps the good stuff alive. And you can just ask the place. Sonar summarizes everything dropped here over the last day and answers — no scrolling. There's no vector database behind this. I'll come back to why. Nothing users post lasts forever. The only permanent pins are sponsored — venues and brands pay for a pin that never expires. That's the business model, and it runs through real billing. Here's the part we're proud of. Sonar is polyglot and all-serverless, two AWS databases each chosen for its access pattern. DynamoDB is the radar — items are keyed by geohash, so "what's near me" is a key query of my cell plus eight neighbors, never a scan. Expiry is a native TTL — but here it carries product logic: the lifespan you pick sets the TTL and the upload byte-cap together, so storage time and storage size trade off in a single choice. And "likes buy time" is an atomic add TTL 300 — the feature is written directly into the storage layer. Streams fan new drops out to WebSocket subscribers and roll usage up for billing. Then Aurora DSQL — serverless, scale-to-zero, Postgres-compatible — is the relational system-of-record: accounts, subscriptions, sponsorships, analytics, with IAM-native auth and a least-privilege role. And because a geofence plus a 24-hour window bound every AI query, the context fits one model call — the data model replaces the vector database. Two databases, each doing what it's best at.
