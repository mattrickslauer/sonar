The problem

Every feed forces one time horizon.

Snapchat forgets everything.
Instagram forgets nothing.
But a place is neither.

A festival, a campus, a downtown block has a living memory of right now. The one question nobody's feed answers:

"Is it worth going over there, right now?"

What it is

A live radar of what's happening around you.

  • Drop a waypoint — note, photo, video, or 15-sec voice — onto a channel.
  • It streams in live, ranked by proximity + freshness, and expires in 24 hours.
  • Channels are real-time, geo-scoped topics you subscribe to.
🎪 Events 🍔 Food 🎶 Music 💬 Social 🛟 Safety
The twist

Likes buy time. Sponsors buy permanence.

⏱ Likes buy time

Every like adds +5 minutes to a drop's countdown — uncapped. The crowd keeps the good stuff alive, minute by minute. Nothing user-posted is ever permanent.

◆ Sponsors buy permanence

The only pins that never expire are sponsored — a paid waypoint carrying the sponsor's name. That's the revenue line, on real billing.

The intelligence

Ask the place.

AI summarizes and answers questions over a location's last 24 hours.

"What's the vibe at the north stage?"
"Where's the shortest food line?"

A geofence + a 24-hour window bound every query small enough for one model call — so there's no vector database.

Why DynamoDB · the radar

The high-write ephemeral path.

  • Geohash partition keys — PK = CH#<channel>#GEO#<geohash6>. "What's near me" = query my cell + 8 neighbors. No scan, ever.
  • TTL is the business logic — 24h expiry is native TTL; "likes buy time" is an atomic ADD ttl 300. The feature is written into the storage layer.
  • Streams fan drops out to subscribers and roll usage up for billing — the DB emits the events; we don't poll.
  • On-demand — absorbs a 30k-person write storm with zero provisioning.
Why Aurora DSQL · the record

The relational system-of-record.

  • Serverless, scale-to-zero — saves ~$88/mo of idle compute vs. Aurora Serverless v2. Right tool and right economics.
  • Several deliberate jobs — accounts (canonical userId), subscriptions/sponsorships, billing & analytics rollups.
  • IAM-native auth — DSQL Signer, no passwords, tokens rotate ~15 min.
  • Least privilege — app role sonar_app: SELECT/INSERT/UPDATE only, no DELETE/DDL.
The decision we're proudest of

The data model is the scaling strategy.

Geohash keys make geo queries O(1). TTL makes ephemerality free. A bounded context deletes the vector database. We picked each store for its access pattern — not the other way around.
